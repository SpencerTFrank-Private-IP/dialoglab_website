## Why there is an AI moderator

Every Dialog Lab conversation includes an AI moderator that stays in the chat and keeps things civil. It steps in when a message crosses the line — but vigorous disagreement about **ideas** is encouraged.

As the app explains: *"Time-limited, anonymous. An AI moderator stays in the chat and keeps things civil — it'll step in if a message crosses the line."*

## Meet your moderator

Effective moderation takes both trust and skill. Our AI moderator:

<ol class="beta-docs__steps">
  <li>Never takes a side — it referees how you argue, not what you believe</li>
  <li>Blocks abusive messages before they're ever sent, so they never touch the conversation</li>
  <li>When it steps in (a nudge, a flag, a suggested rephrase), you both see it and why</li>
  <li>Makes sure you both get your turn</li>
</ol>

## Rules of Engagement

During onboarding, you agree to four simple ground rules. Keep these in mind throughout every conversation:

### Ground Rules

<ol class="beta-docs__steps">
  <li>Assume good intent in your partner</li>
  <li>Respect the conversation style (discussion or debate)</li>
  <li>Labeling or stereotyping will be flagged</li>
  <li>Slurs and abuse will be blocked</li>
</ol>

## Warn vs block

The moderator evaluates every message before it is delivered. Two outcomes are possible:

### Warning

The message **is sent**. A neutral reminder appears to **both** participants — for example, nudging you back to the claims rather than motives. Warnings are shared; neither person is singled out.

### Block

The message is **withheld** and not delivered. Only the sender sees the blocked message and a private explanation of why it was stopped. The chat shows: *"Blocked message from sending."*

| Allowed | Not allowed |
|---|---|
| Strong disagreement on ideas | Personal attacks and insults |
| Challenging your partner's reasoning | Slurs and dehumanizing language |
| Sharing personal experiences | Harassment or threats |

## What you see in chat

Moderator messages appear labeled **AI Moderator**. Some notes are marked **Only visible to you** — these are private coaching or block explanations sent only to the sender.

The moderator may also remind you of ground rules at the start of a conversation and adapt its tone based on your Convo Style preference (casual vs debate).

## Pause anytime

Either participant can send a **Pause** from the chat menu to let the other person know they do not expect to reply for a while. It is a courtesy heads-up — not a freeze on the conversation. Either of you can keep chatting if you want to.

## Reporting vs auto-moderation

The AI moderator handles message-level civility in real time. If your partner's **behavior** is concerning beyond individual messages — harassment, bad faith, or repeated civility violations — use **Report Conversation** from the chat menu or post-chat review. That sends a report to the Dialog Lab team for human review.
