## Pause vs end

From the chat menu (⋯), you have two options:

### Pause

Let your partner know you do not expect to reply for a chosen window — **1 to 24 hours**. This is a courtesy notification, not a lock on the conversation. **Both of you can keep chatting during a pause** if you want to. Use it when you are stepping away but do not want your partner to wonder why you went quiet.

### End

End the conversation permanently. You will be asked to confirm: *"End this conversation? Your partner will be notified and neither of you will be able to send more messages."*

If your partner ends the conversation, you will see: *"Your partner ended the conversation."*

## Post-conversation review

After a conversation ends, Dialog Lab guides you through a short review:

1. **Satisfaction** — rate how the conversation went.
2. **Issues** — note anything that did not work well.
3. **Appreciation note** — optionally share something you valued about your partner (they will see this).
4. **Viewpoint changed** — indicate whether your viewpoint shifted.
5. **Private note** — keep personal reflections only you can see.

This feedback helps Dialog Lab improve matching and moderation over time.

## Reporting from the review

If something went wrong during the conversation, you can **report your partner** from the review screen or from the chat menu during an active conversation. You will be asked to provide details about what happened. The team reviews reports along with the chat log.

Reporting is separate from the AI moderator's automatic warnings and blocks. See [Moderation](/beta/moderation/) and [How to Get Support](/beta/get-support/) for more.

## Finding past conversations

Completed conversations appear in the **Convos** tab. You can revisit the AI-generated summary and your review responses from there.

  <strong>Take your time on the review</strong>
  Honest feedback — even when a conversation was difficult — is one of the most valuable things you can contribute during the beta.
</div>
